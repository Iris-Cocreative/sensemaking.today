An Ethnography of the Present

We are navigating a civilizational inflection point largely in the dark, each holding a flashlight pointed in a different direction.

a companion to james.today
01 — The Gap

The most sophisticated AI systems in history are trained on data that is years out of date.

The world is changing faster than any single observer can track. News is fragmented by ideology and attention economics. No one — not a person, not an institution, not an AI — has a complete picture of what is actually happening right now.

Each of us has a perspective. Each media outlet has a focus. We don't have the hindsight that we will have on these times ten years from now. We're inside the thing, trying to read it from within.

AI can use web search to fill in facts, but it's piecemeal — reading individual articles without the deep contextual tissue, the accumulated sense of momentum and mood that someone living through it has. There's a difference between data points and synthesis. Between information and understanding.

The gap isn't just that AI models are outdated. It's that none of us — human or machine — have access to the kind of collectively-held, real-time understanding of the present that the moment demands.

"There seems a need in the space for more up to date information about where we are as a nation, as a planet, as a species — especially as we grapple with our identity and ideas about our future and how to live together on this planet and how to live together with AI. And that gap should be filled in some way other than simply conducting lots of web searches and reading lots of articles."

This is not a tooling problem. It's a design philosophy problem. We need a live ethnography of the present moment — not more data points, but collective synthesis. And design philosophy is something we can change.

02 — The Vision

A collectively-maintained, living synthesis of right now.

Not a news aggregator, not a social feed, not an archive. Something closer to a shared nervous system: distributed sensing, collaborative sensemaking, and emergent understanding.

A place where the texture of now — the mood, the momentum, the shifting relationships between ideas and events and communities — can be held together. Not by any single author, but by a community of people who trust each other and are committed to seeing clearly.

Not this

Not journalism

Not about breaking news or editorial authority. Not about scoops or takes. About the lived, collective sense of what's shifting — the things that don't show up in headlines but shape everything.

Not this

Not an archive

Not a record of what happened. A living tissue of what's happening — updated, revised, felt into. The primary question is always: what is true now? Present tense.

Not this

Not a platform

No engagement optimization, no algorithmic amplification. No growth metrics as goals. Oriented toward collective intelligence, not attention capture. Small and deep rather than broad and shallow.

I'm not a tree on an island. I'm a pattern that arises in relationship, shaped by everything that came before.
03 — Design Principles

What guides the making

01

Distributed authorship

No single voice holds the narrative. Synthesis emerges from many perspectives held in relationship. The credibility of any contribution comes from the relationships and community context behind it.

02

Present-tense orientation

The primary question is always: what is true now? Not what was, not what will be. The ethnography is of this moment, not the last one.

03

AI as synthesis layer, not author

AI can process, connect, and surface patterns. Humans hold the lived ground truth. The machine helps us see what we collectively know — it doesn't tell us what to think.

04

Epistemic humility as infrastructure

The system should make uncertainty, plurality, and revision visible — not hidden. We don't know what we don't know. That's a feature, not a bug.

05

Circles over grids

Default to radial, relational layouts. Hierarchy is sometimes necessary, but it should be chosen consciously, not inherited from defaults. The shape of the interface shapes the quality of the thinking.

06

Beauty as function

When an interface is genuinely, carefully beautiful — people trust it enough to bring their real thinking. Aesthetics aren't decoration. They lower the walls.

04 — Possible Forms

This could be many things.

The shape hasn't solidified yet. That's intentional. What follows are possible forms, not a roadmap. The right form will emerge from the first conversations, the first contributors, the first attempts at holding a shared picture.

What could this look like?

It's too early to commit to a single form. The important thing is starting — and starting small enough to stay honest.

A curated synthesis A regularly updated, collectively authored document — something between a newsletter and a wiki. A living letter about what's actually happening, written by people who trust each other.
A network of human nodes People embedded in different communities, different geographies, different knowledge domains — each holding a flashlight, together illuminating more of the room.
An AI-assisted sensemaking tool Using Claude or similar systems to process, connect, and surface patterns across contributions. Not AI as oracle — AI as mirror, helping a group see what it collectively knows.
A series of live conversations Regular gatherings — small, trust-based, deep. The sensemaking happens in dialogue, and the artifacts are captured and synthesized afterward.
Visual sensemaking interfaces Using parametric and generative design tools to create real-time visual representations of collective understanding. Concept maps that breathe. Relationship networks that shift with new input.
Community infrastructure Assessment tools, matching systems, interactive directories — the connective tissue that helps communities of practice find each other and work together.
Or all of the above. Probably all of the above, eventually. But not all at once. Start with the conversations. The form will follow the relationships.

06 — Open Questions

What we need to sit with

These questions aren't waiting to be answered. They're waiting to be held — by the right people, in the right conversations. The answers will emerge from the practice, not from planning.

?

Who are the founding contributors and what communities do they represent? What's the minimum viable circle — five people? Fifteen? What level of shared context and mutual trust is needed before the sensemaking is honest enough to matter?

?

What's the minimum viable form — a single shared document? A regular conversation? A group chat? What's the simplest container that can hold real synthesis without collapsing into noise?

?

How does AI enhance the synthesis without colonizing it? When does the mirror become a lens that distorts? How do you keep the machine in service of human understanding rather than replacing it?

?

What's the governance model that keeps this from being captured by any single perspective, ideology, or personality — including James's own? How do you build something collectively authored that feels coherent and alive rather than cacophonous?

?

And the question underneath all the others: can a community of humans, in honest relationship with each other and with AI, hold a shared picture of the present that's more true than any individual's view? Is that even possible? Is that what wisdom is?

The conversation is the product.

Not the platform. Not the tool. Not the framework. The sustained, honest, multi-voiced inquiry into what is actually happening right now — and what it asks of us. Everything else is infrastructure in service of that.

This is a seed document, not a plan. It's meant to be outgrown.
